Steer-by-Wire Security Vulnerability Analysis

Experimental evaluation of sensor-layer attacks in autonomous steering systems

Problem

Steer-by-Wire systems eliminate mechanical steering linkage, relying entirely on sensors and electronic control units. This architectural shift introduces attack surfaces at the sensor and signal level, especially in position encoders.

Approach

Developed a physical-layer attack methodology targeting steering position encoders. Injected controlled disturbances to manipulate steering angle feedback without triggering diagnostics.

System Architecture

• Steering actuator + encoder • Embedded control unit • Signal monitoring framework • Fault injection mechanism

Results

Demonstrated that small sensor biases can produce incorrect steering actuation, potentially destabilizing vehicle trajectory in fully autonomous systems.

Impact

This study establishes a foundation for secure-by-design SbW architectures and highlights urgent safety implications in autonomous vehicles.
